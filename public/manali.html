About Manali

Manali is a scenic high-altitude Himalayan resort town in Himachal Pradesh, India, nestled on the Beas River. Known as a "Valley of Gods", it is a popular destination for tourists, backpackers, and honeymooners, offering snow-capped mountains, dense pine forests, and adventure activities like skiing and paragliding.

Key Aspects of Manali:

Location: Situated in the Kullu Valley, 45 km from Kullu, it serves as a gateway to Lahaul, Ladakh, and Spiti. Best Time to Visit: March to June is ideal for sightseeing (temperatures 10°C–25°C), while October to February is best for snow sports.

Top Attractions: Solang Valley: Popular for zorbing, paragliding, and skiing. Rohtang Pass: A popular, high-altitude spot for snow. Hadimba Devi Temple: A historic temple surrounded by cedar forests. Vashist Village: Known for its hot springs and temples. Old Manali: Famous for cafes and a relaxed vibe. Activities: Trekking (Hampta Pass, Bhrigu Lake), camping, rafting, and visiting the Manu Temple. Shopping: Popular items include Kinnauri and Kullu shawls, Kullu caps, Tibetan artifacts, and local jams.
