About Shimla

Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is a popular Himalayan hill station (elevation >2,000m) and the former summer capital of British India. Known as the "Queen of Hills," it features colonial architecture, the bustling Mall Road, and a historic toy train. Key attractions include the Ridge, Jakhu Temple, and Viceregal Lodge.

Key Information About Shimla

  • Situated in the foothills of the Himalayas, south of the Satluj River.
  • History: Founded by the British in 1819 after the Gorkha War, it became the summer capital in 1864.

    • Important Tips

    Transport: The city is well-connected by road, and the toy train offers a scenic route from Kalka. Accommodation: Areas near the Mall Road are central, while places like Mashobra or Shoghi offer quieter stays. Shopping: The Ridge and Lower Bazaar are popular for handicrafts, woolens, and jewelry
