About Shimla

Shimla, known as the "Queen of Hills," is the capital of Himachal Pradesh and a premier hill station in northern India, situated at an altitude of 2,205 meters. As the former summer capital of British India (since 1864), it boasts colonial architecture, the UNESCO-listed Kalka-Shimla Railway, and scenic Himalayan Geography: Situated in the foothills of the Himalayas, spread across a 12km ridge. Climate: Known for pleasant summers and chilly, snowy winters, making it a year-round destination.
