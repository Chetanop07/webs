Chamba

Chamba is a scenic, historic town in Himachal Pradesh, India, founded in 920 AD by Raja Sahil Varman on the banks of the Ravi River. Known for its 10th-century temples, stunning mountain views (altitudes 2,000–21,000 ft), rich art, and the annual Minjar Mela festival, it serves as a major cultural district. Key Highlights of Chamba: History & Geography: Located in a valley surrounded by, the town is a former princely state known for its ancient history. The area is known for its temperate climate. Culture & Festivities: The famous Minjar Mela is celebrated to commemorate a 935 AD victory over the ruler of Trigarta. Tourism & Architecture: Key attractions include ancient temples and panoramic Himalayan views. Local Cuisine: Known for traditional Himachali food, including Madra, a curd and legume-based dish. Economy: Primarily agricultural, with added, notable industries in craft and pharmaceuticals.
