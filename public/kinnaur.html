Kinnaur

🏔️ About Kinnaur

Kinnaur, created in 1960 and headquartered at Reckong Peo, is a scenic, tribal district in Himachal Pradesh, India, known for its high-altitude mountains (1,600m–6,816m), apple orchards, and a unique blend of Hindu and Buddhist cultures. It lies along the old Indo-Tibetan trade route, featuring stunning valleys like Sangla and villages such as Chitkul. Key Highlights of Kinnaur Geography: Situated in the northeast of Himachal Pradesh, bounded by the Zanskar, Himalaya, and Dhauladhar ranges. Key Destinations: Chitkul: Known as the last inhabited village near the Indo-Tibetan border. Sangla Valley (Baspa Valley): Famous for its scenic beauty and Apple/Apricot orchards. Kalpa: Known for its direct view of the Kinnaur Kailash range. Kinnaur Kailash: A mountain considered sacred to Lord Shiva and Parvati. Culture & People: The region has a distinct cultural identity, with inhabitants known for their hardiness and adherence to tradition. Hinduism and Buddhism coexist, particularly in middle Kinnaur. Activities: Popular for trekking, hiking, birdwatching (e.g., in Rakcham Wildlife Sanctuary), and exploring local temples and forts. Information for Travelers Location: The district is accessible via the old Hindustan-Tibet Road (NH-22). Access: It is approximately 267 km from the nearest airport and 244 km from the nearest railhead, typically via Shimla. Requirements: A valid photo ID is necessary, and for specific treks like Kinnaur Kailash, a medical fitness certificate may be required

📍 Top Attractions

  • Sangla Valley
  • Chitkul Village (Last Village of India)
  • Kalpa Village
  • Reckong Peo

🌤️ Best Time to Visit

April to October (pleasant weather and clear roads).

💡 Travel Tips

Carry ID proof, warm clothes even in summer, and be prepared for long mountain travel routes.
